Ammonia Pipeline Sizing Model

Sizes the liquid NH3 transfer line from required throughput, checks velocity against design limits and the DREAL 650 t/hr flowrate ceiling, and recommends a nominal pipe size and schedule.

BASIS: ASME B31.4 (LIQUID ANHYDROUS NH3)
FLUID TEMP: −33°C (SATURATED, 1 ATM)
REV: DRAFT FOR REVIEW

Feasibility Study mode: quick sizing on fixed engineering assumptions — nominal diameter, schedule, and velocity check only.

Methodology: steady-state mass flow (8,760 h/yr basis) converted to volumetric flow at saturated liquid NH3 density, then sized against nominal pipe IDs per ASME B36.10M to keep velocity within the selected design envelope. Detailed Study adds a Darcy–Weisbach pressure-drop estimate (Swamee–Jain friction factor, commercial steel roughness 0.045 mm) including minor losses from fittings via the sum-of-K method. This is a sizing aid for feasibility/early engineering use, not a substitute for a vendor or EPC line-sizing calculation, stress analysis, or B31.4 wall-thickness/pressure design check.
