Hydrogen Pipeline Sizing Model

Sizes the gaseous H₂ transmission line from required throughput, checks velocity against design limits, estimates pressure drop via Darcy–Weisbach and Weymouth, and recommends nominal pipe size, schedule, and material grade.

BASIS: ASME B31.12 / B31.8 · AIGA 033/15
FLUID: GASEOUS H₂ · ISOTHERMAL
REV: DRAFT FOR REVIEW

Feasibility Study mode: quick sizing on fixed engineering assumptions — nominal diameter, schedule, velocity and pressure-drop check only.

Enter the required H₂ throughput and run the model to see the recommended pipeline size.
Methodology: steady-state mass flow converted to volumetric flow via truncated virial Z-factor (Pitzer correlation), then sized to achieve the target velocity at average line pressure. Pipe selected is the smallest ASME B36.10M NPS that meets both the required ID and the ASME B31.12 hoop-stress wall thickness (t_hoop + corrosion allowance). Pressure drop via Darcy–Weisbach with Colebrook–White friction factor; Weymouth equation (Ef = 77%) included in Detailed Study for comparison. Minor losses via sum-of-K method (D–W) and equivalent pipe length (Weymouth). This is a feasibility/early-engineering sizing aid, not a substitute for a full B31.12 stress analysis, hydraulic model, or material qualification programme.
